What this study is about

In this study, you will complete 10 decision trials. On each trial, you will read a brief gambling scenario and choose between two options. One option involves a known probability, and the other involves an ambiguous probability.

Risks and voluntary participation

  • There are no major risks expected beyond those of everyday decision-making tasks.
  • Some participants may feel mild discomfort when thinking about gains or losses.
  • Your participation is completely voluntary.
  • You may stop at any time without penalty.

Debrief

This study examines whether people are more likely to avoid ambiguity when a gamble is framed as a gain versus a loss. Specifically, it compares choices between a known gamble and an ambiguous gamble in an Ellsberg paradox scenario across multiple trials.
